| time           = 29 minutes}}'''''Grand Canyon''''' is a [[1958]] American short documentary film directed by James Algar and produced by [[Walt Disney Productions]]. It was shown as a supplement during ''[[Sleeping Beauty]]''<nowiki/>'s initial run, and it won an Academy Award at the 31st Academy Awards in 1959 for Best Short Subject (Live Action) It is also included as a bonus feature on the 1997 laserdisc, 2003 DVD, and 2008 DVD & Blu-ray releases of ''Sleeping Beauty''.

According to the opening credits, ''Grand Canyon'' is "a pictorial interpretation of Ferde Grofé's ''Grand Canyon Suite''", much as the animated segments in ''[[Fantasia]]'' are pictorial representations of music, and the film is strongly related to its soundtrack.

The film has no live actors, no dialogue, and no narration, only musical accompaniment.
