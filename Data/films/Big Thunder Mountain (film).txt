==Production==
Attempts to adapt the Big Thunder Mountain Railroad attraction trace back to 2013, when ABC ordered a pilot for a series adaptation starring [[Ryan Reynolds]], but was ultimately scrapped.<ref>[https://www.deadline.com/2013/01/big-thunder-mountain-drama-mark-gordons-gothica-get-abc-pilot-orders/ UPDATE: Big Thunder Mountain Drama, Projects From Mark Gordon, Ryan Reynolds & Martin Campbell Get ABC Pilot Orders]</ref>

On August 16, 2022, ''Deadline'' reported that A film adaptation of the ride entered development, with siblings Michelle and Kieran Mulroney set to write the script for Walt Disney Pictures, with LuckyChap Entertainment and Scott Free Productions co-developing the film. Directing duo Bert and Bertie were set to helm the project; the two were hired after presenting their pitch to Disney; the duo had previously established a positive relationship with the company through their work as episode directors for the Disney+ series ''[[Hawkeye (series)|Hawkeye]]'' (2021), with the studio impressed "from the start" with their vision for the film.<ref>[https://deadline.com/2022/08/bert-bertie-big-thunder-mountain-disney-luckychap-and-scott-free-1235093441/ ‘Hawkeye’ Directors Bert & Bertie To Direct Big Thunder Mountain Movie For Disney, LuckyChap and Scott Free]</ref>
