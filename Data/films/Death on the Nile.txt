'''''Death on the Nile''''' is an American mystery thriller film. It is directed by [[Kenneth Branagh]], written by Michael Green, and based on the 1937 novel of the same name by Agatha Christie. The film is a sequel to the [[2017]] film, ''Murder on the Orient Express'' and features Kenneth Branagh as [[Hercule Poirot]], along with an ensemble cast that includes [[Gal Gadot]], [[Letitia Wright]], [[Armie Hammer]], [[Annette Bening]], Ali Fazal, [[Sophie Okonedo]], Tom Bateman, Emma Mackey, Dawn French, Rose Leslie, Jennifer Saunders, and [[Russell Brand]]. It is the third film adaptation of the novel after the 1978 film and the 2004 episode of the television series ''Agatha Christie's Poirot''.

==Synopsis==
[[Hercule Poirot]] is assigned with a mission to find a killer while on a vacation in Egypt.

==Reception==
On the review aggregator website Rotten Tomatoes, 66% of 190 critics' reviews are positive, with an average rating of 5.90/10. The website's consensus reads, "Old-fashioned to a fault, the solidly entertaining ''Death on the Nile'' is enlivened by its all-star cast and director-star Kenneth Branagh's obvious affection for the material." Metacritic, which uses a weighted average, assigned the film a score of 52 out of 100 based on 50 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".
