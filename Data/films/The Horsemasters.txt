'''''The Horsemasters''''' was a 2 part episode of the ''Disneyland'' TV show from [[1961]] which screened theatrically in some countries. It was shot on location in [[England]].<ref>Hedda Hoopper, 'Walt Disney Will Film Romantic Teen Comedy', ''Chicago Daily Tribune'' ([[1923]]-[[1963]]) [Chicago, Ill] [[August 20]], [[1960]]: w17.</ref>

== Plot ==
A group of young people do a course in horsemanship in [[England]].

== Production ==
It was based on a [[1957]] novel by Don Stanford. Film rights were bought by [[Walt Disney]], who authorised a television movie version to be shot in [[England]]. Disney made a number of films in England around this time, including [[Greyfriars Bobby|''Greyfriars Bobby'']]. In August [[1960]], [[Annette Funicello]] and [[Tommy Kirk]] were cast.

The musical number, "Strummin' Song", performed by Annette Funicello and written by the [[Sherman Brothers]] marked the first song the Sherman Brothers ever wrote for a [[Walt Disney Productions|Disney]] project.

The film was shot on location in England. Filming started September 1960.
