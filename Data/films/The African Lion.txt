'''''The African Lion''''' is a [[1955]] feature-length documentary by Walt Disney Productions as part of its [[True-Life Adventures|''True-Life Adventures'']] series. The film, which was shot over a three year period focuses on the life of the lions within the complexity of the Africa ecosystem. At the 6th Berlin International Film Festival it won the Silver Bear (Documentaries) award. It was released on DVD in [[2006]] as part of the Walt Disney Legacy Collection. It can be found on the third volume of the ''True-Life Adventures'' series where it has been fully restored.

*''The African Lion'' is often cited as an influence on the [[Jungle Cruise]] attraction.
*After the scene with the rhino stuck in the mud was shot, the filming team rescued the animal, but it charged at them after being freed.
