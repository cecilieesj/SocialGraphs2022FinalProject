| Trivia                  = * One of the creative differences why ''[[Deadpool (film)|Deadpool]]'' director [[wikipedia:Tim Miller (director)|Tim Miller]] left the movie was his idea to incorporate into the plot a deeper exploration of the love story between [[Vanessa Carlysle (Earth-41633)|Vanessa]] and [[Wade Wilson (Earth-41633)|Wade]], which included her transformation into the super-powered [[Copycat]]. In the last moments prior to exiting the project, Miller spoke to 20th Century Fox higher-ups and pleaded they kept that aspect of the movie even if they scrapped the rest.<ref name="Playlist">{{cite web|url=https://theplaylist.net/tim-miller-deadpool-2-fantastic-four-20191029/|title=Tim Miller Reveals His Plans For His Unmade ‚ÄòDeadpool 2‚Äô Including A Battle Featuring ‚ÄòFantastic Four‚Äôs‚Äô The Thing|work=The Playlist|first=Griffin|last=Schiller|date=29 October 2019|accessdate=31 October 2019}}</ref>
** The relationship between Deadpool and [[Russell Collins (Earth-41633)|Russell]] being the heart of the movie was planned from the start, even before Miller left production.<ref name="Playlist"/>
** Miller intended for Cable to be more comic accurate and be 6'7" tall.<ref name="Playlist"/>
** Miller had also gotten permission to make use of [[Fantastic Four]] character [[The Thing]] and have him fight the [[Cain Marko (Earth-41633)|Juggernaut]] in the film's climactic action set-piece.<ref name="Playlist"/>
* The film's earliest drafts set it roughly five years after the first film, and featured Deadpool as a father. According to Ryan Reynolds himself, "by page 1.5, it was totally untenable" and the idea was dropped.{{r|{{cite web|url=http://ew.com/movies/2018/05/07/deadpool-2-original-script/|title=Deadpool 2 original script had Deadpool as a dad|work=Entertainment Weekly|first=Tim|last=Stack|date=7 May 2018|accessdate=8 May 2018}}}}
* Some drafts of this film had Wade and Vanessa breaking up, rather than her dying.<ref name="CBR interview">{{cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/deadpool-2-spoilers-screenwriters-rhett-reese-paul-wernick-interview/|title=Deadpool 2 Writers Break Down the (Many) Big Surprises in the Film|work=CBR.com|first=Albert|last=Ching|date=18 May 2018|accessdate=19 May 2018}}</ref>
* Deadpool was almost going to sport an updated costume with additional paneling on the arms, back, and legs. This redesign was dropped in favor of continuing with the costume from the first movie.{{r|{{cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/deadpool-2-ryan-reynolds-unused-costume/|title=Ryan Reynolds Shares Pic of Unused Deadpool 2 Costume|work=CBR.com|first=John|last=Ridlehoover|date=13 July 2020|accessdate=15 July 2020}}}}
* The X-Men's cameo was directed by ''[[Dark Phoenix (film)|Dark Phoenix]]'' director Simon Kinberg. The characters were filmed in that film's set in Montreal, and then they were inserted in ''Deadpool 2'' via green screen.{{r|{{cite web|url=http://comicbook.com/marvel/2018/05/18/deadpool-2-x-men-cameo-scene-professor-beast/|title=Simon Kinberg Directed That Hilarious 'Deadpool 2' Scene|work=ComicBook.com|first=Brandon|last=Davis|date=18 May 2018|accessdate=18 May 2018}}}}
* Black Tom originally had a bigger role in an earlier script of this film. He lived until the end of the third act, and he became the devil on Firefist's shoulder, trying to get him to turn bad. His role was cut due to budget constraints.<ref name="CBR interview"/>
* Brad Pitt's cameo as the Vanisher was worked on very late into the film's production. According to co-writer Rhett Reese, he agreed to do it "for scale and a cup of Starbucks ‚Äî but Ryan's got to go get the Starbucks for me."<ref name="CBR interview"/>
* The film's mid-credit scenes were shot as part of additional photography, and were not featured in test screenings.<ref name="CBR interview"/>
* The film's mid-credit scenes of Deadpool travelling through time originally ended with Wade killing an infant Adolf Hitler. According to co-writer Rhett Reese, this idea was dropped due to the belief it was too harsh to have Deadpool kill a baby.{{r|{{cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/deadpool-2-ending-kill-hitler-baby/|title=Deadpool 2 Nearly Ended with Wade Killing Baby Hitler|work=CBR.com|first=Stephen|last=Gerding|date=18 May 2018|accessdate=18 May 2018}}}} An altered version of this scene was included in the ''The Super Duper Cut'' home release of the film, with Deadpool instead calming him, changing his diaper and saying that he will be back with Cable as he loves killing kids.
