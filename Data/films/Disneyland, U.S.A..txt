'''''Disneyland, U.S.A.''''' is a documentary film that originally released to theaters by [[Buena Vista Distribution]] on [[December 20]], [[1956]] in a double bill with ''[[Westward Ho, the Wagons!]]'' The narrator ([[Winston Hibler]]) gives us a tour of the [[Disneyland]] theme park as it was in its earliest form.Â 

It was released on DVD as part of the [[Walt Disney Treasures]] set ''Disneyland: Secrets, Stories and Magic''.

==Summary==
''Disneyland, U.S.A.'' begins its tour with a helicopter overview of the entire park, before landing at the [[Disneyland Heliport]] and making a brief visit to the [[Disneyland Hotel]].

From there, the film is an extended tour of the park, including:
*[[Main Street, U.S.A. (Disneyland)|Main Street USA]], which features a [[Main Street Vehicles|horse-drawn street car view]] of the area.
*[[Frontierland (Disneyland)|Frontierland]], with a ride on the [[Mine Train Through Nature's Wonderland|Rainbow Caverns Mine Train]] and a trip on [[Rivers of America]] aboard the [[Mark Twain Riverboat]] and ending on a visit to the Indian Village.
*A trip on the original [[Jungle Cruise]].
*[[Tomorrowland (Disneyland)|Tomorrowland]], featuring the [[Autopia]], Astro-Jets, and model jet demonstrations. Aerial views also show the fabled Phantom Boats in action.
*[[Fantasyland]], with exterior teases of the dark rides and a trip on the [[Storybook Land Canal Boats]].
*A parade on Main Street and a flag retreat ceremony, with the film ending on a "night view" of the park.
When the film first premiered at Radio City Music Hall in New York, [[Disneyland USA at Radio City Music Hall|a special introduction]] featuring [[Walt Disney]] talking about the construction of the park played.
