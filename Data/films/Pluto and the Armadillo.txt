'''''Pluto and the Armadillo''''' is a [[1943]] animated short, starring [[Mickey Mouse]] and [[Pluto]].

==Plot==
[[Mickey Mouse|Mickey]] and [[Pluto]] arrive in [[South America]]. While there, Mickey throws Pluto's ball and Pluto goes after it. While looking for it, Pluto mistakes the ball for an armadillo that has just rolled itself up. Eventually, Pluto makes friends with the armadillo and chases him through a cave. After a while, Pluto mistakes the ball for the armadillo and thinks he's ripped it up until the armadillo reappears unharmed.

==Trivia==
*This cartoon marks the final appearance of Mickey in his trademark red two-button shorts and does not wear any shirt during his cartoon series' original theatrical run. The shorts would not be seen again in animation until ''[[Mickey's 60th Birthday]]'' in [[1988]].
*This cartoon was inspired by the Disney artists' goodwill tour of Latin America and was originally set to be included in ''[[Saludos Amigos]]'' accompanied by a live-action segment on a Brazilian zoo. Some footage of an animator drawing the armadillo, as well as a pencil test of the armadillo's walk, is featured in the documentary ''[[South of the Border with Disney]]''.Â
