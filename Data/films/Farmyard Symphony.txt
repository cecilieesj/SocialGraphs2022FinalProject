'''''Farmyard Symphony''''' is a ''[[Silly Symphony]]'' that debuted on [[October 14]], [[1938]] inspired by ''[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farmyard_Song Farmyard Song]''.

==Synopsis==
The daily routine of the farm animals, set to music. The high point is a rooster serenading a chicken, with all the animals joining in. But then comes the sound that's even more welcome to the animals: the farmer and his wife with food.

==Music==
Classical music excerpts heard in this short, in order of appearance:
*[[wikipedia:Symphony No. 6 (Beethoven)|Symphony No. 6 in F major, Op. 68, Mvt. III: Allegro]] by [[wikipedia:Ludwig van Beethoven|Ludwig van Beethoven]]
*''"Ranz des vaches"'' from [[wikipedia:William Tell Overture|William Tell Overture]] by [[wikipedia:Giachino Rossini|Giachino Rossini]]
*"Allegretto grazioso: Frühlingslied" aka "Spring Song", one of [[wikipedia:Felix Mendelssohn|Felix Mendelssohn]]'s [[wikipedia:Songs Without Words|Songs Without Words]]
*"[[wikipedia:The Campbells are Coming|The Campbells are Coming]]", a traditional Scottish march
*Rossini's ''The Barber of Seville''
*''[[wikipedia:La donna è mobile|La donna è mobile]]'' from the opera ''[[wikipedia:Rigoletto|Rigoletto]]'' by [[wikipedia:Giuseppe Verdi|Giuseppe Verdi]]
*Finale from Rossini's William Tell Overture
*Friska from [[wikipedia:Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2|Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2]] by [[wikipedia:Franz Liszt|Franz Liszt]]
*Overture from the opera ''[[wikipedia:Tannhäuser (opera)|Tannhäuser]]'' by [[wikipedia:Richard Wagner|Richard Wagner]]

==Trivia==
*Footage from this short would later be used in ''[[Golden Eggs]]'', ''[[Chicken in the Rough]]'', and ''[[Lady and the Tramp]]''.
*The Limited Gold Edition Silly Symphonies VHS used the burlap opening titles and the original United Artists closing titles, and the later burlap titles were seen on the ''Extreme Music Fun'' DVD.
*The opening credits music, "[[The Pastoral Symphony]]", would be reused in ''[[Fantasia]]'' two years later.
