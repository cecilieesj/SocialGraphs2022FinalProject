'''Tattypoo''' is the white-haired Good Witch of the North from ''The Wizard of Oz''. In the original novel, she was unnamed. She was referred to as "Locasta" in the 1902 stage adaptation, but this name was not used for the character anywhere else in the ''Oz'' franchise, especially the "Oz" novels, having appearing sometimes as a one-joke character. Her name "Tattypoo" was finally revealed in the twenty-second ''Oz'' novel, ''The Giant Horse of Oz''. In the 1939 well-known [[Metro Goldwyn Mayer|MGM]] film adaptation, the Good Witch of the North was removed her role was played by [[Glinda the Good Witch of the South|Glinda]].

Tattypoo was played by [[Miss Piggy]] (voiced by [[Eric Jacobson]]) in ''[[The Muppets' Wizard of Oz]]''. She likes [[Munchkins (Muppets' Wizard of Oz)|the Munchkins]] very much and enjoys coming in for a cuddle or two. She also likes [[penguins]]. Tattypoo and her sisters, [[Evanora the Wicked Witch of the East|the Wicked Witch of the East]], [[Theodora the Wicked Witch of the West|the Wicked West of the West]] and [[Glinda the Good Witch of the South]] were once a pop singing group entitled the Four Little Pigs.
