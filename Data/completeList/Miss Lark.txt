|quote ="If you and your family should ever need a place to stay, Willoughby and I would be happy for the company."|status=Active}}'''Miss Lark''' is a minor character in ''[[Mary Poppins (film)|Mary Poppins]]'' and [[Mary Poppins Returns|its sequel]]. She's an elegant woman who has a dog named Andrew. At the beginning of the original film, [[Bert]] mentions that "Miss Lark likes to walk in the park with Andrew," which shows the reason for her presence there during his musical performance as a one-man band.

==Background==
===Behind the scenes===
Sudha Bhuchar made her Disney feature film debut as Miss Lark in ''Mary Poppins Returns''. As a Tanzanian-born British Asian actor, playwright, and cofounder of the Tamasha Theatre Company, she is best known for writing numerous screenplays in television and film.

==Role in the films==
===''Mary Poppins''===
Miss Lark is seen watching Bert's performance. As he approaches her, he gazes upon Andrew before he continues performing in front of everyone just as they leave after his good performance. They witness a strong wind across Cherry Tree Lane just as [[George Banks|George]] and [[Michael Banks|Michael]] notice [[Mary Poppins (character)|Mary Poppins]] arriving on the street. She isn't seen again for the rest of the film afterward.

===''Mary Poppins Returns''===
Miss Lark appears briefly appears during the part where [[Annabel Banks|Annabel]], [[Georgie Banks|Georgie]], and [[John Banks|John]] hang out at the park just as Georgie mingles with her pet dog, Willoughby, while he sees a [[The Balloon Lady|balloon lady]] from a distance. She later appears when Willoughby goes after the runaway kite that Michael used to own. Furthermore, she appears during the part where everything else is being packed up just as she says goodbye to Willoughby. She later appears during the musical number "[[Nowhere to Go But Up]]" alongside Willoughby while they float via balloon.
