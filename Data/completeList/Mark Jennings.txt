Hinton's novel|alias = The Hitchhiker|personality = Dangerous, cruel, murderous, greedy|occupation = Drug dealer|alignment = Bad|goal = To kidnap Tex and Mason (failed)|home = Tulsa, Oklahoma|enemies = [[Tex McCormick]], [[Mason McCormick]]|likes = Money, selling drugs|dislikes = Not getting what he wants|weapons = His gun|fate = Gets shot to death by the police officers}}'''Mark Jennings''' is the main antagonist in the [[1982]] live-action film, ''[[Tex (film)|Tex]]''.

==Role in the film==
On their way to Bixby, [[Tex McCormick|Tex]] and [[Mason McCormick|Mason]] pick up Mark and he points a gun into Mason's chest and instructs Tex to drive him to the state border. To get the attention of a following police car, Tex swerves off the road while Mason grabs the gun out of Mark's hands. Mark tries to escape, but the police officers manage to shoot him dead.

Seeing the boys on that evening's television news report, their father Pop McCormick telephones to announce his return from the rodeo. He arrives early the next morning and is berated by Mason for his negligence as a parent.

==Trivia==
* The hitchhiker didn't have a first name in the novel.
* He is unrelated to one of the protagonists from ''That Was Then, This Is Now'', which is another novel written by S.E. Hinton.
* His name was only mentioned by the newscaster after his death and is simply credited as "Hitchhiker".
